Eden Prairie MN Community Profile

Staincrete is proud to offer its decorative concrete services to the people of Eden Prairie Minnesota.  We thought you might like to know a bit more about this interesting and vibrant community.

History of Eden Prairie

Originally, the early settlers of the area where Eden Prairie is located were Native Americans.  In 1851, a treaty allowed land west of the Mississippi River (where Eden Prairie is located) for pioneers to settle. The first Eden Prairie Town Hall meeting was held on May 11, 1858, which interestingly was the same day where Minnesota was declared a state.  The town’s name was originally Eden in 1853 a name coined by a Mrs. Eliot who selected it due to her admiration of the “beautiful prairie” located at the southern end of the community.

The affordability of the housing market in Eden Prairie, MN as well as the overall cost of living can be indicated accurately by what percent of the monthly income is spent on housing related costs. This is konwn as the debt-to-income ratio (DTI). The overall rule of thumb is that no more than 28% of your gross income should ever be used on the overall costs related to housing and no more than 36% of gross income on all expenses and debts. Among Eden Prairie, MN homeowners, the median income is $115,397. This indicates that a monthly maximum of $2,692 should be spent by homeowners on all housing related costs. Renters have a median income of $61,326. This indicates that renters should have a maximum of $1,431 used for their rent related monthly costs.

Schools in Eden Prairie

The Eden Prairie school district manages 8 K-12 schools, 6 elementary schools and one middle school.  Eden Prairie High School is the 2nd biggest in the state. There is one private school and one publicly funded charter school in the city.

Private Schools

International School of Minnesota  — founded in 1985 and offers college prep education from preschool through grade 12.
