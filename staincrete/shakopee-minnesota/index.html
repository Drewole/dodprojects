Shakopee MN History

The city is named after Chief Shakopee of the Dakota tribe. Thomas Holmes established the trading post in 1851 and platted the village in 1854. The small settlement quickly grew and by 1857 incorporation to cityhood reached. The Dakota war saw it surrendering its charter in 1861 but regained incorporation in 1870 when tensions lifted.

Fun Things to Do in Shakopee MN

Canterbury Park – Want to try your luck at horseracing? Visit the this horse race track and card club situated at the intersection of Canterbury Road.

The Landing – is an 88-acre historic settlement that is situated on the Minnesota River. If you want to see how people lived in the 1840s-1890s this is the place to go.

Valley Fair – bring the kids to this family amusement park just outside Shakopee downtown.
