History of Chanhassen

The origin of the name Chanhassen is from the Dakota, which means “sugar maple tree.” The earliest settlers of the town arrived in the 1850s to look at farms. The first claim that was settled happened in 1852 where a German by the name of Joseph Vogel, for the area west of Rice Lake near the Shakopee station. When the deal with the Native American Indians ended in 1853, more settlers came to live in the small settlement. The first town meeting was held on the 17th of May 1858 and after the Civil War ended, the Rev. Magnus Mayr officially a parish for German Catholics. The establishment of the Milwaukee and St. Paul Railroad brought more settlers and incorporation into the Village of Chanhassen happened in 1896.

One can approximate the cost of living and the housing affordability in Chanhassen, MN by the percentage of a person’s income that he or she spends on the monthly housing, otherwise known as the debt-to-income ratio (DTI). To stay on the safe side and keep one’s head above the water, a consumer should never spend more than 28 percent of his or her gross income on housing-related expenses and costs and not more than 36 percent of income on all expenses and debts. Homeowners in Chanhassen, MN have a median income of $120,043. Therefore, the amount that a consumer should spend on housing costs should be no more than $2,801 per month to avoid calamity and poverty. The renters in the city have an income of about $45,000, which would make their monthly maximum allowable rental costs $1,050.

Fun Things to Experience in Chanhassen MN

The city is proud to offer residents and visitors with a number of amenities for recreation such as open spaces, parks and trails.

Beaches

The city has five public beaches namely:

  • Lake Ann Beach
  • Greenwood Shores Beach
  • Minnewashta Regional Park
  • Roundhouse Park Beach
  • Carver Beach

Skate Park

If skating is to your liking the city offers The Chanhassen Skate Park. Installed in September of 1999 it is located between City Hall and the fire station downtown. The park offers a quarter pipe, hotbox; grind rails, doobie roller, a half-pipe and a wedge.

Trails

The city prides itself on offering residents and visitors with a multipurpose trail system for recreation. The city has 70 miles of beautiful trails.
