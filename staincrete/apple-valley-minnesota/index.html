Apple Valley MN Community Profile

Staincrete is proud to offer its decorative concrete services to the people of Apple Valley Minnesota.  We thought you might like to know a bit more about this interesting and vibrant community.

History of Apple Valley

The original name of Apple Valley was Lebanon Township.  It was founded in 1969 by a real estate mogul by the name of Oris Thompson.  He studied where in the Twin Cities will there be an area where good and consistent growth can be expected. He was assisted by a company from Apple Valley California, hence the name.  The area that was decided upon was one and a half mile from County Road 42 and Cedar Avenue.

If you want to find a good determining factor for the cost of living and the general affordability of housing in Apple Valley, MN, all you must look at is the percentage of monthly income that should be spent on housing expenses, otherwise known as the debt-to-income ratio (DTI). Generally speaking, no more than 28 percent of your total gross income should be spent on your housing expenses and not more than 36 percent of your gross income on total expenses, including debts. In Apple Valley, MN, the median monthly income is $91,907 for those who own their own homes. Under the standard rule, that income level would allow for $2,144 to be budgeted for housing expenses each month, at the most. Renters, on the other hand, earn about $43,631, per the median income in the area. That would allow for renters to spend up to $1,018 on monthly rental expenses.

Schools in Apple Valley, MN

The city is served by Independent School District 196 and is comprised of six elementary schools, three middle schools and three high schools. There is also a magnet school that serves 11th and 12th grade students. Other magnet schools include: Cedar Park Elementary which follows a science, technology, engineering and math (STEM) curriculum. Other students also attend schools from other districts as Minnesota follows an open enrollment statute.

Restaurants & Nightlife

Vivo Kitchen Restaurant – considered the best restaurant in the city according to Trip Advisor, they offer a good whiskey and wine selection as well as pizzas and flat breads.  There is a nice patio section for alfresco dining. The wedge salad with Parmesan shrimp is quite fabulous.  Service is friendly and accommodating.

Taste of Thaiyai Restaurant – Excellent Thai meals that is easy on the pocket. Do try the Thai iced tea as well as the veggie egg rolls. The Rad Na with mock duck is absolutely fabulous.

Osaka Seafood Steakhouse Restaurant – Don’t let its strip mall location prevent you from trying out this gem of a restaurant. Food and service is excellent. Steak and salmon dinner or with shrimp is delightful.  The sushi is also quite fabulous as well. Try the tempura roll and the spicy snow crab roll.
